## Tue Jul 15 — LinkedIn Post

**Source:** Defensible theses — governance
**Pillar:** Governance / NHI
**Remix mode:** none (original thesis)

```
Your next breach won't have a face.

Every AI agent you deploy is a non-human identity. It has credentials. It has permissions. It can act on systems. And in most orgs, it has no manager, no review, and no entry on the org chart.

We spent 20 years building identity governance for humans — joiners, movers, leavers, segregation of duties. Then we handed system access to software that spins up faster than HR can blink, and decided governance was someone else's problem.

Segregation of duties doesn't stop mattering because the employee is a model.

If you're deploying agents, you need three things before you need a bigger model: an owner for every agent, least-privilege by default, and an audit trail you'd hand to a regulator without flinching.

The breach is coming. The only question is whether you can answer "who approved this?"
```
