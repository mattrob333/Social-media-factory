Anthropic shipped Claude Cowork to mobile and web yesterday.

That sentence undersells what changed.

Cowork tasks used to die when you closed your laptop. Now they run in the cloud — persistent, scheduled, cross-device. You start a client prep at your desk, close the lid, and Claude finishes while you're in the air.

This is the "harness over model" thesis playing out in real time.

The model wasn't the bottleneck for agent viability. The runtime was. You needed a desktop open and a browser connected. Anthropic just removed that constraint, and the ceiling on what "set it and forget it" means moved up by a lot.

90% of Claude sessions are already non-coding. Mobile Cowork is aimed precisely at that majority — operators, not engineers. The people who need to say "draft the QBR deck for the 2 pm" from the airport gate.

Two implications worth watching:

1. Scheduled execution with no device online is the feature nobody asked for until they had it. That's the cron-job-for-agents moment. I've been saying the cron job is the most underrated tool in AI — this is Anthropic baking it directly into the product.

2. The control surface just changed. When your agent can work for hours unattended, the question isn't "does it work?" anymore. It's "what happens when it makes a decision you didn't anticipate?" That's where the governance layer (scoped permissions, audit trails, spend ceilings) becomes the product, not the nice-to-have.

Cowork on mobile makes agents a real-time utility. The bottleneck now is trust — not capability.
