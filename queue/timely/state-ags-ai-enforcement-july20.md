State AGs don't need new AI laws to regulate your agent.

Reuters today: 42-state AG coalition already formed. Colorado's automated-decision law is enforceable as consumer protection. Texas used data privacy law against an insurer's AI driving-behavior database. Pennsylvania sued Character.AI for AI bots posing as medical professionals.

The federal government won't pass comprehensive AI legislation this year. Maybe next. The states aren't waiting.

If you're deploying agents, the enforcement tools that matter are 50 existing consumer protection acts — not an AI bill that doesn't exist. A deceptive trade practice claim doesn't change because the algorithm that denied the loan is a model, not a human.

This is the governance gap nobody in the AI hype cycle is talking about. You're building permission structures for your agents, but state AGs are building enforcement structures you didn't plan for.

Segregation of duties doesn't stop mattering because the employee is a model. And consumer protection doesn't stop applying because the decision was automated.
