@steipete asked a question that's settling the agenda: "Are we still talking loops or did we shift to graphs yet?"

He's right. Loop engineering made agent behavior programmable. Graph engineering makes agent organizations programmable.

A single agent in a loop can brute-force a lot. But production at scale needs more than one agent iterating on one task. It needs specialized agents wired together — sequential, parallel, conditional, with handoffs and fallbacks.

That's the Shadow Org pattern. Not one agent. An org chart of them, each with clear ownership, limited scope, and a defined escalation path.

The shift from loops to graphs is the shift from writing subroutines to designing teams. And teams need governance, not just prompts.

The labs are shipping better models. The real leverage is in how you organize them.
