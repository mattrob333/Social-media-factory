Google shipped three new Gemini models today. All Flash tier. Faster, cheaper, built-in computer use.

The headline: 3.6 Flash at $7.50/M output, 17% fewer tokens than 3.5 Flash, computer use as a native client-side tool. 3.5 Flash-Lite at 350 tok/s for $2.50/M output.

But the real story is what didn't ship.

3.5 Pro missed its third deadline. Google is testing it with "partners" and hoping to "land soon." Meanwhile OpenAI shipped GPT-5.6, Anthropic has Sonnet 5 and Fable 5, and Google's flagship hasn't updated since February.

The Flash models are genuinely good — DeepSWE 49%, OSWorld 83%, MLE Bench 63.9%. At those prices, the economics of agentic workflows shift. You can afford to loop more, retry more, parallelize more.

But the flagship gap is widening, and Google's response is to keep shipping cheaper lane-splitters. That works for volume. It doesn't work for the frontier.
