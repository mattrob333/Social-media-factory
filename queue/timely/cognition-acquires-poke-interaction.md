Cognition just acquired Poke — the AI agent that lives in your texts, approved on Apple Messages, with 100M+ messages exchanged in the last 3 months.

On the surface: Devin (enterprise SE agent) buys a consumer messaging frontend.

Underneath: this is a bet on interface as moat. Poke's edge isn't a better model. It's the UX design — proactive, conversational, phone-native. People don't chat with a chatbot. They text with Poke.

The acquisition pair is a signal: the winners in agents won't be the ones with the best model. They'll be the ones who make the agent feel like a person you'd actually text.

That's a harness problem, not a model problem. And Microsoft's MAI blog today proves the same thing from the other direction — train inside the product stack, on your own workflows, and you beat frontier models on the tasks that actually matter.

The interface is the moat. The model is the commodity.
