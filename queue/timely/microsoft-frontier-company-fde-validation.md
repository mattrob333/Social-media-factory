Microsoft just put $2.5B and 6,000 people behind "forward-deployed engineering" — embedding their own engineers inside customers to actually build and run the AI, not just sell the model.

Notice the timing. Amazon committed $1B to the same thing two days ago. OpenAI and Anthropic both stood up their own deployment ventures in May. Four of the biggest AI companies on earth just independently concluded the same thing in eight weeks: the model was never the bottleneck. Getting it to actually work inside a real company's data and workflows is.

That's the whole thesis. It just took four trillion-dollar companies to agree with it.

Here's what they can't buy their way out of: sending 6,000 people to embed with customers isn't forward-deployed engineering, it's consulting with better branding. The entire value of FDE is one or two engineers who deeply understand both the system and the business sitting inside the account until it ships. Scale that model and you've recreated the org chart it was invented to route around.

The gap it opens isn't smaller because Microsoft entered it. It's bigger — because now enterprises have seen the pitch and know to ask "how many people are actually touching my system," not "how big is your AI division."
