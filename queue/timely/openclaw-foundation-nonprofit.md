OpenClaw is now a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, MIT licensed, with a full-time team and backing from OpenAI and NVIDIA.

Six months ago this was a weekend project in Austria. Now it's the fastest-growing repo in GitHub history and a foundation with a governance charter.

This is what maturity looks like for open-source AI: the moment the community realizes "we need someone to hold the keys, and it can't be a VC or a founder."

The lobster now has a board. Every personal AI project that wants enterprise trust will need to follow this arc — open license, neutral steward, proper governance. You can't sell NHI into compliance-driven orgs without it.

Well played, claw people.
