Sen. Warner just dropped the AI AGENT Act — the first federal legislation to take agentic AI seriously. FTC registry, fiduciary duties for agents, platform interoperability.

The framework is smart. It gives consumers the right to choose their own agent instead of being locked into whatever agent the platform gives them. If you want a shopping agent that works for *you* on Amazon rather than Alexa that works for Amazon, you get one.

The blind spot? It's all consumer-side. Shopping agents, travel booking, social media assistants. The Act doesn't touch the enterprise — where agents already have credentials, permissions, and direct access to production systems, and nobody can answer "who owns this agent?" or "what was the last action it took?"

Consumer agent choice is the right conversation to start. But the bigger governance gap is inside every Fortune 500 that's already deployed agents with no identity, no owner, and no rollback plan.

You don't need a federal registry. You need an org chart entry for every non-human identity, a clear chain of accountability, and an audit trail you'd hand to a regulator without editing.

Segregation of duties doesn't stop mattering because the employee is a model.
