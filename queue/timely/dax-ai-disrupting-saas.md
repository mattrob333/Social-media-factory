dax just said what every forward-deployed consultant sees happening but nobody talks about.

The AI job disruption conversation is about individual roles. The real disruption is to the companies themselves. We're throwing out software we've used for years — not because AI is cheaper, but because connecting AI to raw data gives a fundamentally better experience.

Most SaaS products are managed databases with a UI tax. AI skips the UI and talks to the data directly. The middle layer evaporates.

This is what I mean when I say the next 1,000 AI winners are vertical plays for boring industries. Not because building is hard — because the existing software incumbents are sitting on a rent that AI is about to obsoletе.
