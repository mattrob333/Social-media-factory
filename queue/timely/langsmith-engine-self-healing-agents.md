LangChain's new "LangSmith Engine" scans your agents' failures, prioritizes them, drafts fixes, and runs continuously. No human in that loop by design.

That's not an agent watching agents. That's a non-human identity with commit rights to your other non-human identities, and no manager over either one.

Autonomous triage is fine. Autonomous patching of production behavior is where segregation of duties stops being optional — because now the thing deciding what "fixed" means is the same system category that broke it.

Ship the diff, don't ship the deploy. Human approves the patch. Every time. That's not friction, that's the product.
