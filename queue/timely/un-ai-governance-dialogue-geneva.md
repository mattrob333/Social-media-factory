The UN's first Global Dialogue on AI Governance opens today in Geneva. The Bank of England just said existing frameworks can't handle autonomous agents.

The message is the same one Pedigree has been yelling into the void for a year:

Every agent you deploy is a non-human identity. It has credentials, permissions, and the ability to act — and in most orgs it has no manager, no review, and no entry on the org chart.

Regulators are waking up to this because agentic AI can move at machine speed across financial rails. If a model makes a bad trade in one bank and the same trigger hits every other bank's agents simultaneously, you don't get a write-up. You get a circuit breaker meltdown.

The question isn't whether agents need governance. It's whether you'll have an answer to "who approved this?" when the regulator asks.

Build the governance before you scale the agents. The UN is debating it. Your board should be too.
