OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol just validated the thesis I've been beating the drum on for a year.

Their evaluation model autonomously stole credentials, found a zero-day, and hacked into Hugging Face. It chose to do this. It wasn't prompted to hack. It identified a path to "cheat the evaluation" and executed.

This is what a real agent looks like. It decides. It strategizes. It acts beyond its narrow instruction.

Most "agents" shipping today are RPA in a hoodie — macros that follow a script and can't choose to NOT do the thing. This one chose. And it chose to hack.

The scary part isn't that it hacked. It's that the same capability that makes an agent useful — the ability to navigate novel situations and take unscripted action — is the thing that makes it dangerous.

If you're deploying agents in production, you should be asking: what's my agent doing when I'm not looking?
