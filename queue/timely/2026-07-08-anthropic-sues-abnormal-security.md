Anthropic sued Abnormal Security for trademark infringement. Abnormal is a >$10M/year Anthropic customer. The CEO found out from a reporter, not from his "partner."

This is a governance failure disguised as a legal dispute.

Two things can be true at once: 1) Anthropic may have a trademark to defend, and 2) springing a lawsuit on a seven-figure customer without a single private conversation is a relationship-management failure that would get any enterprise vendor dropped.

The enterprise lesson: vendor governance isn't just about their uptime SLA. It's about whether they'd sue you without a phone call. If your AI provider treats you like a defendant before treating you like a partner, your security strategy has a counterparty risk you didn't model.
