Your next enterprise AI deployment just got a new prerequisite.

Tomorrow, OpenAI launches GPT-5.6 Sol to the public — its strongest model yet, alongside Terra (matching GPT-5.5 at half the cost) and Luna (budget tier at $1/$6 per M tokens).

But the launch mechanism is the real story.

The Trump administration requested pre-release government review under the June AI cybersecurity order. OpenAI sent technical experts to DC. The Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation ran additional testing. Only after clearing that process did OpenAI get permission for a wide launch.

If you're deploying AI in any regulated industry, this is the test case you need to understand. The question is no longer just "does this model perform." It's "who has approved this model for use, and under what conditions?"

Segregation of duties doesn't stop mattering because the model is a black box. And if the US government can ask for pre-deployment review of a frontier model, your enterprise vendor can answer for their own deployment pipeline too.

The clearance process is becoming part of the product — whether anyone planned for it or not.
