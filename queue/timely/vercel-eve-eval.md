Vercel's eve ships built-in evals: "Web frameworks made testing an ecosystem choice. For agents, evals are essential."

This is the right call. React's bet that testing could be an add-on worked because a broken render is cheap. A broken agent crosses accounts, files, deploys, and permissions. The blast radius is different.

Every agent framework that launches without evals baked in is shipping the same mistake as shipping React without a testing story — except the mistake now costs money, not a console error.

Evals aren't a feature. They're the product boundary between "demo" and "deployment."
