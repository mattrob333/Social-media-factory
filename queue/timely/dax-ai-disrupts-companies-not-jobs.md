Everyone's still asking "will AI take my job?" The better question is "will my employer survive AI?"

We're junking SaaS products we've used for years — not because they're too expensive, but because hooking an agent to raw data gives a better experience than the dashboard ever did.

Most B2B software is a managed database with a UI tax. When you don't need the UI, you don't need the company.

The AI layoff narrative has it backwards. It's not about headcount. It's about the companies that were built on data friction suddenly having none.
