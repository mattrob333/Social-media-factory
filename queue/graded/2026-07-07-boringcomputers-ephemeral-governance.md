"Spin up a computer, hand it to an AI agent, get a working app back, then it self-destructs."

That's boringcomputers.com — open-source, by @Rasmic. It's the kind of project that sounds like a gimmick until you think about what it implies.

Ephemeral compute + agent + self-destruct is the right pattern for 90% of agent use cases. You don't want state you can't explain. You don't want an agent that persists its mistakes. You want a sandbox that lives exactly as long as the task and dies clean.

Most agents being deployed today are the opposite: persistent, accumulating context debt, and nobody knows what state they're in.

The next governance norm isn't "audit the agent." It's "the agent was never there to begin with."
