Someone built "Council of High Intelligence" — 18 personas (Aristotle, Feynman, Kahneman, Torvalds, etc.) across multiple LLM providers, deliberating on hard questions via one /council command.

The persona theater is the least interesting part. The real idea underneath: genuine model diversity, not one model prompted 18 different ways.

Ask GPT-5.6 to argue with itself and it converges fast — same training data, same blind spots, same failure modes wearing different hats. Ask three different providers the same question and you get three genuinely different error surfaces. Disagreement becomes signal instead of noise.

That's the piece worth stealing for any high-stakes call — a manifest decision, a go/no-go, a legal read. Route the same question through providers that were actually trained differently, then force them to disagree before you trust the answer.
