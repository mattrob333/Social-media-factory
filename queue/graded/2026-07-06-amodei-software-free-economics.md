Dario Amodei: software goes to ~zero marginal cost. Value moves to whoever wields it best.

True, but incomplete. The real bottleneck won't be wielding the tool — it'll be who can make the output reliable enough to bet on.

Free code has no warranty. An AI agent that books a wire transfer or emails a client is worthless the moment nobody can say who approved it. The value doesn't move to the prompter — it moves to whoever builds the audit trail: who owns the agent, what it can touch, and who signs off when it's wrong.

The model got cheap. Accountability is still expensive.
