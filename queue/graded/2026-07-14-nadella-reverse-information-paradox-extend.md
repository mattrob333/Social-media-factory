Nadella just named the problem elegantly: the Reverse Information Paradox.

Enterprises pay for AI twice — once in cash, once in the proprietary know-how their prompts, corrections, and evals leak with every query.

He's right. But his solution (proprietary learning environments on cloud) is half the answer.

The real fix: own your context layer, not your model.

The harness — your evals, your prompts, your institutional memory, your feedback loops — decoupled from any single model. That's what compounds. The model is a commodity that swaps out. The context is the moat.

If your AI strategy hands your evals and corrections to the same vendor selling you tokens, you're not buying intelligence. You're renting your own institutional knowledge back to yourself at margin.

Stop feeding the model your alpha. Build the boundary.
