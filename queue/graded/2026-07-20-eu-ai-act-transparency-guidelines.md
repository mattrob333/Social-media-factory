The EU just published the AI Act transparency guidelines. Effective August 2 — 13 days from now.

If you deploy agents in Europe, three things kick in:

1. Every chatbot must identify itself as AI on first interaction
2. AI-generated content must carry machine-readable detection marks
3. Deepfakes on public-interest matters require disclosure

The timing is the story. The same week agent deployment is accelerating across every vertical, the world's most comprehensive AI regulatory framework goes live. "Move fast and break things" doesn't work when the regulator has enforcement powers.

The orgs that navigate this cleanly will have done the work before the deadline — governance baked in, not bolted on after the fine. The orgs that scramble will be the ones who treated AI compliance as a future problem.

Segregation of duties doesn't stop mattering because the regulator is elected.
