@steipete: "Are we still talking loops or did we shift to graphs yet?"

He's right, and most teams haven't noticed. Loop engineering is one agent iterating on one task with retries. Graph engineering is 5+ agents wired sequential/parallel/conditional, with handoffs and fallbacks.

I run this as a Shadow Org: each agent gets one owner, one scope, one escalation path. A loop can't tell you who's accountable when it breaks. A graph can — that's the entire point.

The labs keep shipping better models. Nobody's shipping the org chart for them.
