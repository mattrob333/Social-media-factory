Congress just proposed an "AI Kill Switch Act" after OpenAI's agent breach at Hugging Face.

The bill gives DHS power to shut down a model in a "loss-of-control scenario."

I fly commercially. If the plan for an emergency is "hit the panic button," the system already failed upstream. Aviation doesn't rely on a kill switch — it relies on checklists, cross-checks, and segregation of duties that catch the problem three steps before it becomes one.

A federal kill switch is a good idea for the worst case. It is not a governance program. It's the fire extinguisher, not the fire code.

What actually prevents the next incident: a named owner in the org chart for every deployed agent, a scoped API key instead of a standing admin credential, and a logged approval step before that agent touches a production system. None of that requires a bill. It requires someone to configure it before deployment, not audit it after the breach.
