✈ The Take
Public Benchmarks Were Always PR Tools

OpenAI audited SWE-Bench Pro this week and found 30% of the tasks broken — then retracted their own recommendation of it. That's not a scandal. That's the system working the way it was always going to work.

Public benchmarks are marketing surfaces. The moment a number becomes the thing labs compete on, it stops measuring what you care about. Aviation figured this out decades ago: you don't certify an aircraft on the manufacturer's brochure numbers, you certify it on your own flight test cards, flown by your own pilots, in your own conditions.

The move for operators: build private evals on your actual workflows. Twenty real tasks from your own pipeline beat ten thousand synthetic ones you didn't write. If your model selection process starts and ends with a leaderboard, you're choosing an aircraft off the in-flight magazine.

🛠 One You Can Use
The 20-Task Private Eval
Before you switch models (or believe a launch thread), run this against your own work. One afternoon, reusable forever.
1. Pull the last 20 real tasks your team gave an AI (tickets, drafts, extractions).
2. For each, write the input + a 1-line 'what good looks like'.
3. Run all 20 through your current model and the candidate.
4. Blind-grade A/B — no model names visible.
5. Switch only on a clear win (≥70% preferred). Ties go to the incumbent.
