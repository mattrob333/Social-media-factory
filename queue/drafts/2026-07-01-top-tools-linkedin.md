I looked for the ten best AI tools this week. I found four.

Most "10 AI tools you need to know" lists are padding. A given week rarely produces ten genuinely new things — usually two or three, plus a pile of me-too repos chasing the same trend. This week, four things earned an operator's attention:

OmniRoute (https://github.com/diegosouzapw/OmniRoute) — a free, self-hostable AI gateway that routes across 231+ model providers (50+ free), with automatic fallback and 15-95% token compression. Model routing, the thing companies were charging for a year ago, just became a config file. I'm evaluating it as a cost and fallback layer inside client agent deployments — not replacing my existing routing, adding redundancy underneath it.

Hermes (Nous Research) — the agent runtime this business runs on, including the system that drafted this post. Matthew Berman, one of the most-watched AI tutorial channels on YouTube, just walked his audience through it (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TML-0HmxWCE) and called it "the best thing since OpenClaw." I don't need to pitch you on Hermes. An outside voice with no stake in my business just did, independently.

video-use (https://github.com/browser-use/video-use), from the browser-use team — the same "agent drives the app's real interface instead of a custom integration" pattern that made browser automation agentic, now applied to video editing. +722 stars in a day. It's the clearest signal yet that agentic tool-use is generalizing past browsers into every category of software, including content ops.

agents-cli (https://github.com/google/agents-cli), from Google — the official CLI for creating, evaluating, and deploying agents on GCP. The cloud vendors are now racing to own the "agent deployment surface," the same fight they had over Kubernetes distributions a decade ago. Not a build priority for me, but a real data point if your enterprise clients are already GCP-native.

Two of these I'm actually using. Two are watch-list. The honest version of a "top tools" list says which is which — otherwise it's just content marketing wearing a numbered list.

Most "top 10" lists exist to hit a round number. The tools worth your time this week: four.
