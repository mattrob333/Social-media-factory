# LinkedIn Post Template

The loop uses this as a format reference when generating LinkedIn posts.

---

## LinkedIn Format

LinkedIn posts are longer than X posts. They reward storytelling, structure,
and professional framing. Best-performing posts combine personal experience
with actionable takeaways.

### Structure

**Hook (first 2 lines — what shows before "see more")**
- Start with a surprising fact, question, or story opener
- Make the first line strong enough to earn the click
- Avoid: "I'm excited to announce..." or "Thrilled to share..."

**Body (3-5 short paragraphs)**
- Tell a story or walk through a framework
- Each paragraph: 2-3 sentences max
- Use bullet points for lists (LinkedIn formats them well)
- Single line breaks between paragraphs (LinkedIn shows more in feed)

**Close (1 paragraph)**
- One clear takeaway
- Optional: question to drive comments
- No hashtags in the close — they go on their own line

**Hashtags (separate line at the bottom)**
- 2-3 relevant hashtags
- No more than 5

### Example

```
I spent 3 years building a product nobody wanted.

Here's what I learned about finding product-market fit the hard way:

Year 1: Built in secret. Launched to silence.
I spent 11 months coding before showing it to anyone. When I finally
launched, I got 12 signups. Zero paid conversions.

The mistake: I was solving a problem I assumed existed.

Year 2: Talked to 50 potential customers.
Every conversation revealed the same thing — the problem I was solving
was #4 on their priority list. It mattered, but not enough to pay for.

The real pain point? Something completely different. Something I'd
overlooked because it seemed "too simple."

Year 3: Rebuilt around the real problem.
Launched a stripped-down version in 6 weeks. Charged from day one. Hit
$10K MRR in 4 months.

Three lessons I wish I'd learned sooner:

• Talk to customers before writing a single line of code
• Charge from day one — free users don't give real feedback
• Simple solutions to urgent problems beat complex solutions to minor ones

What's one assumption about your market you haven't validated yet?

#ProductMarketFit #StartupLessons #BuildInPublic
```
