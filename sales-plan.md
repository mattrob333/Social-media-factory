# Revenue Operating System — Daily Checklist

A portable daily plan for outbound sales, pipeline nurturing, and content-driven
inbound. Built for an operator-pilot who flies 18 days a month and needs
systems that run on the other days.

---

## The Three Revenue Channels

| Channel | What it is | Time to money | Effort |
|---------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| **1. Warm outbound** | Your existing network, past clients, partners (Wesco, Tier 4) | Days-weeks | Low |
| **2. LinkedIn content → inbound** | Posts establish authority, prospects reach out | Weeks-months | Medium |
| **3. Cold outbound** | Direct outreach to regulated industry buyers | Weeks | High |

**Start with Channel 1 today. Channel 2 runs on autopilot (content calendar is built). Channel 3 starts week 2.**

---

## Monday — Pipeline Push

### Morning (1 hour)

- [ ] **Send 3 warm outreach messages.** These go to people you already know.
  See [Warm Outreach Templates](#warm-outreach-templates) below.
  - 1 to a past client: "How's the AI rollout going? We built something new."
  - 1 to a partner (Wesco, Tier 4): "What are your clients asking about AI? Let's co-sell."
  - 1 to a conference connection: "Saw your post about X. We're solving that at Pedigree."

- [ ] **Post today's content.** Grab from `queue/approved/` — it's already written.
  - Monday = X thread day

- [ ] **Engage on LinkedIn for 15 minutes.** Comment on 5 posts from target buyers.
  Not "great post!" — add a real insight. You're building a paper trail of thinking.

### Afternoon (30 min)

- [ ] **Follow up on last week's outreach.** Anyone who replied? Move them to a call.

---

## Tuesday — LinkedIn + Follow-up

### Morning (45 min)

- [ ] **Post today's LinkedIn content.** Tuesday = LinkedIn post day.

- [ ] **Send 2 DM follow-ups.** People who viewed your profile or engaged with posts.
  See [Engagement Outreach](#engagement-outreach).

- [ ] **Review Monday's outreach replies.** Any "interested"? Send a Calendly link.

### Afternoon (30 min)

- [ ] **Research 5 target accounts.** Pick 5 companies in regulated industries
  (healthcare, insurance, financial services, aerospace, manufacturing).
  Find the person responsible for AI/innovation/security. Save to a CRM or spreadsheet.

---

## Wednesday — Cold Outreach + Authority

### Morning (1 hour)

- [ ] **Post today's content.** Wednesday = X single or thread.

- [ ] **Send 5 cold outreach messages.** See [Cold Outreach Templates](#cold-outreach-templates).
  Target the accounts you researched Tuesday. Keep it short. Lead with the problem.

- [ ] **Pitch one podcast.** Find one podcast your buyers listen to. Send the host a DM:
  "I fly a CRJ 18 days a month and build AI governance the rest. Happy to talk about
  non-human identity and why your next breach won't have a face."

### Afternoon (30 min)

- [ ] **Review pipeline.** How many conversations are active? Who needs a nudge?

---

## Thursday — Content + Partnerships

### Morning (45 min)

- [ ] **Post today's content.** Thursday = X single.

- [ ] **Partner outreach.** Send 2 messages to potential channel partners.
  See [Partner Outreach](#partner-outreach).

- [ ] **Engage on LinkedIn for 15 minutes.** Target regulated industry conversations.

### Afternoon (30 min)

- [ ] **Follow up on Wednesday's cold outreach.** Anyone opened? Send a gentle nudge.

---

## Friday — Pipeline Review + Planning

### Morning (30 min)

- [ ] **Pipeline review.** Count:
  - Conversations active: ___
  - Calls scheduled: ___
  - Proposals out: ___
  - Closed (this month): ___

- [ ] **Send "Friday nudge" to anyone who went quiet.** See [The Friday Nudge](#the-friday-nudge).

### Afternoon (20 min)

- [ ] **Weekly retrospective.** What worked? What didn't? Adjust next week's plan.

- [ ] **No new outreach on Friday.** People are checked out. Use this time for
  strategy, not volume.

---

## Saturday-Sunday — OFF

Content posts are scheduled Mon-Thu. No outreach on weekends. The cron jobs
run without you. You're either flying or recharging.

## Outreach Templates

### Warm Outreach Templates

**To a past client:**
```
Subject: AI governance — we shipped something new

Hey [Name],

Been thinking about our work on [project]. We just shipped a governance
layer for AI agents — non-human identity, audit trails, least-privilege
access. The thing we wished we had back then.

If your team is deploying agents, I'd love to show you what we built.
20 minute demo?

- Matt
```

**To a partner (Wesco, Tier 4):**
```
Subject: Your clients are asking about AI governance

[Name],

What are your enterprise clients saying about AI deployment? We're seeing
a pattern: they want to deploy agents but can't answer "who approved this?"

Pedigree solves that — non-human identity management + audit trails.
We're looking for forward-deployed partners who are already in the building.

Want to co-sell? I'll do the product demo, you bring the relationship.
```

**To a conference connection:**
```
Subject: That conversation about [topic] stuck with me

Hey [Name],

We talked about [topic] at [conference]. I've been building in that exact
direction — Pedigree handles AI governance for regulated industries
(healthcare, insurance, aerospace).

Would love to catch up and show you what we built. 15 minutes?

- Matt
```

## The Unfair Advantage

You're an airline captain who ships AI. Nobody else has this.

Use it in every conversation. Not as a gimmick — as proof you understand
systems that can't fail. Aviation CRM. Checklists. Audit trails. The cost
of quiet failure. When you talk about AI governance, you're not theorizing.
You're translating 90 years of safety engineering into software.

Your cold outreach hook: "I fly a CRJ 18 days a month and build AI governance
the rest. Same job — both fail quietly, both punish skipping the checklist."

That gets replies.
